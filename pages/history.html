History

Timeline

657 BCE — Byzantium founded by Greek colonists from Megara (named after leader Byzas).

330 CE — Constantine I designates the city as capital of the Roman Empire.
Renamed Constantinople. Massive building program begins: city walls, forums, Hagia Sophia (original).

537 CE — Current Hagia Sophia completed under Justinian I. Remains largest cathedral for nearly 1000 years.

1054 — Great Schism divides Christianity into Catholic (Rome) and Orthodox (Constantinople).

1204 — Fourth Crusade sacks Constantinople. City looted; Latin Empire established.
Venetians take much of the spoils now in St. Mark's Basilica.

1261 — Byzantine Empire restored under Michael VIII Palaiologos.

29 May 1453 — Ottoman Sultan Mehmed II ("the Conqueror") takes the city.
Hagia Sophia converted to mosque. City renamed İstanbul (though this predates conquest in usage).
Becomes capital of the Ottoman Empire.

1520–1566 — Reign of Suleiman the Magnificent. Peak of Ottoman power.
Süleymaniye Mosque built by architect Mimar Sinan.

1609 — Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmed Mosque) completed.

1853 — Topkapı Palace abandoned; Dolmabahçe Palace built in European style on Bosphorus.

1908 — Young Turk Revolution limits the Sultan's power.

1914–1918 — WW1. Ottoman Empire fights alongside Central Powers. Gallipoli campaign.

1923 — Treaty of Lausanne. Turkish Republic proclaimed. Capital moved to Ankara.
Atatürk's modernization program: Latin alphabet, secular law, women's suffrage (1934).

1934 — Hagia Sophia converted to museum by Atatürk.

2020 — Hagia Sophia re-converted to mosque. Museum status removed.
