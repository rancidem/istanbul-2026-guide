Personal Picks

Recommendations from people who've done the research.

Legend: [*] = Mom's pick · [**] = Mom's top priority · [d] = Anna's pick

Mom's Top Priorities [**]

These are the non-negotiables from your mother's planning notes.

  • Asian Side (Kadıköy, Moda, Üsküdar) — Local markets, little shops, small restaurants. Far less touristy than the European side. Full half-day minimum.
  • Asian Side Turkish Breakfast — Kadıköy/Moda area. Sat or Sun, 10am–12:30pm. Do this.
  • Asian Side Street Food Tour — Daily 12pm–3pm or 5pm–8pm. Kadıköy food scene.
  • Grand Bazaar — 4,000+ shops. Open 9am–7pm, closed Sunday. Bargain — start at 50% of asking price.
  • Spice Bazaar / Egyptian Market — 10am–7pm, Eminönü. Best for spices, teas, dried fruits.
  • Topkapı Palace — Go at opening (9am) to beat crowds. Buy tickets online. Don't skip the Harem (separate ticket). Closed Tuesday.

Mom's Other Recommendations [*]

Sights

  • Hagia Sophia — Buy tickets online. €25 upper gallery. 9am–7pm.
  • New Mosque (Yeni Cami) — Near Spice Bazaar, Eminönü. Free. Beautiful tiles, far less crowded than Blue Mosque.
  • Balat & Fener — Colorful houses, antique shopping, Church of St. George. Combine with Kariye Museum (Chora Church) nearby.
  • Arasta Bazaar — Sultanahmet. Upscale crafts: quality textiles, ceramics, carpets. Less chaotic than Grand Bazaar.

Experiences

  • Bosphorus Cruise + Breakfast — Combine sightseeing with traditional breakfast on the water. Mon/Tue/Sun, 9–11am.
  • Mezze Cooking Class — 2pm–6pm (4 hours). Sun or Mon. Learn to prepare traditional Turkish mezze.
  • Soccer Match — Sun 8–10pm. Turkish football culture is an experience.
  • Live Music — Beyoğlu or Kadıköy. Turkish classical, fasıl, jazz.

Hammams

  • Kılıç Ali Paşa Hamamı — Tophane (near Karaköy). Sinan-designed. Luxury. Women until 4pm.
  • Cağaloğlu Hamamı — Near Hagia Sophia. Open until 10pm. Good for first-timers.
  • Süleymaniye Hamamı — Near Süleymaniye Mosque.

Food

  • Cafe Privato (Beyoğlu) — Turkish breakfast spread.
  • Mesopotamian Terrace Restaurant (Sultanahmet) — Breakfast with a view.

Practical

  • Istanbulkart — Get this at the airport. 130 TL (refundable card). Covers metro, trams, ferries, buses. Essential.
  • SIM Card — Alosim recommended. Or Turkcell / Türk Telekom at airport. Bring passport.

Anna's Picks [d]

Must-See

  • Kadıköy — Youthful, vibrant, authentic. Quirky antique shops, vintage stalls, colorful lanes, endless cafes. Oldy-worldly trams. Live music. The best neighborhood on the Asian side.
  • Ortaköy Mosque — Open to non-Muslims. Waterway setting, gorgeous ceiling. Free entry, photos allowed.
  • Dolmabahçe Palace — Turkey's largest palace. World's largest ballroom. ~€6. 2–3 hours. Nearby: Yıldız Park, Taksim. Free alternative: just view the gardens and entrance archways.

Day Trips

Anadolu Kavağı [d]

Where the Bosphorus meets the Black Sea

Ferry from Istanbul. Quiet fishing village, very different from the tourist core.

  • Yoros Castle — Hilltop ruin with striped round towers. Panoramic views of the Black Sea entrance.
  • Colorful houses, seafood restaurants, serene misty landscape
  • The ferry ride itself is part of the experience — passes under the suspension bridges

Good for: budget day out, food, photography.

Princes' Islands [d]

No cars. Peaceful escape.

Ferry from Kabataş or Kadıköy (~1 hour). Four islands: Büyükada, Heybeliada, Burgazada, Kınalıada.

  • Büyükada — Largest. Victorian mansions, historic churches, waterfront cafes. Rent a bike.
  • Pine forests, quiet lanes, good swimming in summer
  • Go on a weekday to avoid crowds

Bursa [d]

First capital of the Ottoman Empire, 2 hours away

Ferry + bus from Istanbul. Called "Green Bursa" for its parks and gardens.

  • Grand mosques, mausoleums, energetic bazaars
  • Uludağ Mountain — Cable car, skiing in winter, hiking in summer
  • Best in spring or autumn
