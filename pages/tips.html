Tips & Practical Info 2026

Visa Requirements

  • Many nationalities need an e-Visa — apply at evisa.gov.tr (official site only)
  • Cost: ~$50 USD
  • Valid: 90 days within 180-day period, multiple entry
  • Tip: Get it before arrival — airport is slower and same price

Money & Currency

  • Currency: Turkish Lira (TRY)
  • Inflation: Ongoing — verify current rates before travel
  • Cards: Accepted at most restaurants, shops, museums
  • Cash needed for: Street food, small eateries, some ferries, tips
  • ATMs: Widely available; use bank ATMs (avoid standalone machines)
  • Tipping: ~10% at restaurants appreciated but not mandatory
  • Exchange: Avoid airport and Sultanahmet; better rates at Grand Bazaar area
[TIP] 2026 Update: Many museums now quote prices in Euros (€) and accept EUR cash. This eliminates currency confusion but verify current rates.

SIM Card & Connectivity

  • Providers: Turkcell, Vodafone, Türk Telekom
  • Where to buy: Airport (most convenient), official stores in city
  • Requirements: Passport for registration
  • Typical cost: ~€10-15 for SIM + €15-20 for 20GB data package
  • Duration: 30 days
  • Tip: Register the SIM or it will deactivate in days

Turkish Letters

  • ş = sh (as in "ship")
  • ç = ch (as in "church")
  • ğ = silent (lengthens preceding vowel)
  • ı = undotted i (sounds like "uh")
  • ö = like German "ö"
  • ü = like German "ü"

Safety & Scams

General Safety

Istanbul is generally safe for tourists. Comparable to major European cities. Exercise normal big-city awareness.

Common Scams to Avoid

ScamHow It WorksAvoidance
Shoe-shine Someone "drops" a brush; if you pick it up, they insist on cleaning your shoes for inflated price Don't pick up dropped items
"Turkish hospitality" Friendly stranger invites you for drinks; bill arrives with outrageous charges Decline invitations from strangers
Wrong change Shopkeeper gives incorrect change hoping you won't count Always count your change
Currency trick Shopkeeper processes transaction in EUR without asking Specify currency before paying
Free food Unordered dishes appear at table; you're charged for them Send back anything you didn't order

Petty Theft Hotspots

  • Grand Bazaar and Spice Bazaar
  • İstiklal Avenue
  • Taksim Square
  • Crowded tram stations

Prevention: Keep bags in front in crowds; don't keep wallet in back pocket

Mosque Etiquette

  • Remove shoes before entering (watch where others put theirs)
  • Women: Cover head, shoulders, knees (scarves usually provided at entrance)
  • Men: No shorts; cover shoulders
  • Visiting hours: Avoid prayer times (especially Friday 12:30-14:30)
  • Behavior: Speak quietly, no photos during prayers
  • Entry: Free at all active mosques

  • BiTaksi — Licensed taxi booking
  • Trafi — Public transport navigation (best for Istanbul)
  • Yemeksepeti — Food delivery
  • Getir — Quick delivery (groceries, essentials)
  • Google Translate — Camera translation for menus/signs

What to Pack

  • Comfortable walking shoes — Cobblestones and hills everywhere
  • Headscarf — For women visiting mosques (also provided at entrances)
  • Layers — Mornings cool, afternoons warm; unpredictable weather
  • Portable charger — You'll use your phone for maps and photos
  • Power adapter — European Type F (220V)
  • Sunscreen — Even in spring/autumn
>> Final Tip: Istanbul rewards patience. Don't try to see everything. Take the slow ferry. Sit with tea. Let the city work on you. The magic happens in the in-between moments.
